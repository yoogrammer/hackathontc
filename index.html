Got Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is CodeVerse? +
CodeVerse is an offline hackathon organized by Tuljaram Chaturchand College (TC College), Baramati, Maharashtra. It's a 2-day event where student teams build innovative solutions to real-world problem statements in 10-12 hours.
Who can participate? +
CodeVerse is open for students from all colleges. You need to form a team of 2-3 members. Solo participation is not allowed. Cross-college teams are welcome!
How much is the registration fee? +
The registration fee will be disclosed soon. Registration is paid and will be done through a Google Form. Registration remains open until the hackathon starts.
When will problem statements be revealed? +
Problem statements will be revealed at the start of the hackathon on Day 1 (28th). Teams will then have the first 30 minutes to finalize their ideas and get approval from judges before starting development.
What technologies can we use? +
The theme is Open Innovation, so you can build solutions using any technology - websites, mobile apps, AI/ML, chatbots, LLMs, blockchain, IoT, or any other technology that aligns with the problem statement. Open source libraries and APIs are allowed.
Will we get certificates? +
Yes! All participants will receive certificates of participation. Winners will receive additional recognition along with prizes.
Will mentors be available during the hackathon? +
Yes, mentors and volunteers will be available throughout the event to provide guidance and technical support. They'll help you overcome challenges and make the most of your hackathon experience.
What should we bring to the hackathon? +
Bring your laptop, chargers, valid student ID, and any hardware/devices you might need for your project. Also bring your creativity, problem-solving skills, and team spirit!
How will projects be judged? +
Projects will be evaluated based on innovation, technical implementation, problem-solving approach, user experience, presentation quality, and potential real-world impact. Judges' decisions will be final.
